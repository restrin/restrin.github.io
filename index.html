About

Hello! I'm a fifth year Ph.D. student at the Institute for Computational and Mathematical Engineering. I graduated with my B.Sc. from the University of British Columbia with a Combined Honours in Mathematics and Computer Science.

My research is largely focused on developing and analyzing algorithms for large-scale computation, such as iterative solvers for linear systems and algorithms for large-scale optimization. Some specific interests include:

  • Iterative methods for linear systems
  • Preconditioning
  • Algorithms for convex and non-convex optimization
  • Inexact computations

I am also a member of Computational Consulting (C2).

Aside from academics, I've been playing tennis and practicing Taekwondo for several years.

My funding is generously provided by the Gene Golub Fellowship Award from ICME.
